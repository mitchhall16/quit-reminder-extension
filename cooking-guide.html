Simple Cooking Guide

Easy, healthy meals. No experience needed.

Before You Start

You need: a pan or baking sheet, cooking oil or butter, salt, pepper. That's it. Everything else is optional. Start simple, get good at the basics, then add more.

Fresh > Meal Prep

All these recipes are meant to be made fresh and eaten right away. 10-20 minutes max. No sad fridge leftovers. Cook what you need, eat it hot.

Perfect Chicken Breast
25
Minutes
400¬∞F
Oven
What You Need
  • 1 chicken breast
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (or any oil)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Garlic powder (optional)
Steps
  1. Turn on your oven to 400¬∞F. Let it heat up while you prep.
    If your oven beeps when ready, wait for that. If not, give it 10-15 mins.
  2. Take the chicken out of the package. Pat it dry with paper towels. Wet chicken = no crispy outside.
  3. Put chicken on a baking sheet or oven-safe pan. Drizzle oil on both sides. Sprinkle salt, pepper, and garlic powder on both sides.
  4. Put it in the oven. Set a timer for 20-25 minutes.
    Thicker breast = 25 min. Thinner = 20 min. When in doubt, cut the thickest part - no pink inside means it's done.
  5. Take it out. Let it sit for 5 minutes before cutting. This keeps it juicy.

Simple Pork Chop
15
Minutes
Med-High
Stovetop
What You Need
  • 1-2 pork chops (bone-in or boneless)
  • 1 tbsp butter or oil
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Garlic powder (optional)
Steps
  1. Take pork chops out of the fridge 15-20 min before cooking. Room temp meat cooks more evenly.
  2. Pat them dry with paper towels. Season both sides with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  3. Put a pan on the stove. Turn to medium-high heat. Add butter or oil. Wait until it's hot (butter will bubble, oil will shimmer).
  4. Place pork chops in pan. DON'T TOUCH THEM for 4-5 minutes. Let them get a brown crust.
    If they stick when you try to flip, they're not ready. Wait another minute.
  5. Flip them. Cook another 4-5 minutes on the other side.
  6. Check if done: cut into the thickest part. Slightly pink in center is OK (145¬∞F). No red.
  7. Let rest 3-5 minutes before eating.

Ground Beef (for anything)
12
Minutes
Med-High
Stovetop
What You Need
  • 1 lb ground beef (80/20 or 90/10)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Garlic powder
  • Onion powder (optional)
Steps
  1. Put a large pan on medium-high heat. No oil needed - beef has enough fat.
  2. Add ground beef to the hot pan. Break it up with a spatula or wooden spoon.
  3. Keep breaking it into smaller pieces as it cooks. Stir every minute or so.
    Smaller pieces = more browning = more flavor.
  4. Cook until no pink remains, about 8-10 minutes.
  5. Drain the fat if there's a lot (tilt pan, spoon it out). Season with salt, pepper, garlic powder.
  6. Use for: tacos, rice bowls, pasta, or just eat it with vegetables.

Eggs (3 Ways)
5-10
Minutes
Med
Stovetop
Scrambled Eggs
  1. Crack 2-3 eggs into a bowl. Add a splash of milk (optional). Beat with a fork until mixed.
  2. Put a non-stick pan on medium heat. Add a little butter.
  3. Pour eggs in. Wait 30 seconds, then gently push eggs from edges to center with a spatula.
  4. Keep gently pushing until eggs are just set but still slightly wet. Take off heat - they'll finish cooking from the heat of the pan.
  5. Season with salt and pepper.
Fried Eggs
  1. Put a non-stick pan on medium heat. Add butter or oil.
  2. Crack egg directly into pan. Don't break the yolk.
  3. Cook until whites are set but yolk is still runny (about 3-4 min). For over-easy: flip and cook 30 more seconds.
  4. Season with salt and pepper.
Hard Boiled
  1. Put eggs in a pot. Cover with cold water by 1 inch.
  2. Bring to a boil. Once boiling, turn off heat and cover pot.
  3. Let sit 10-12 minutes.
  4. Put eggs in cold water or ice bath for 5 minutes. Then peel.

Easy Rice Bowl
20
Minutes
One Pan
Full Meal
What You Need
  • 1 cup rice (any kind)
  • 1 lb ground beef or chicken
  • 1 bag frozen vegetables
  • Soy sauce
  • Salt, pepper, garlic powder
Steps
  1. Cook rice according to package. Usually: 1 cup rice + 2 cups water, bring to boil, then simmer covered for 15-20 min.
    Or just buy microwave rice packets. No shame in that.
  2. While rice cooks, brown your meat in a pan (see ground beef recipe above).
  3. When meat is almost done, add frozen vegetables directly to the pan. Cook until heated through (5 min).
  4. Add 2-3 tbsp soy sauce to the pan. Stir everything together.
  5. Put rice in a bowl. Top with meat and veggie mixture.

Ginger Honey Tea
10
Minutes
Healing
Good For
What it looks like: Light golden/yellow liquid, slightly cloudy. Ginger pieces floating or strained out. Steam rising.
What You Need
  • 1-2 inch piece of fresh ginger
  • 1-2 tbsp honey
  • 2 cups water
  • Lemon (optional)
Steps
  1. Cut ginger into thin slices or small chunks. No need to peel - just wash it.
    More ginger = stronger/spicier. Start with less if you're new to it.
  2. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot or kettle.
  3. Add ginger slices to the boiling water. Reduce heat to low. Let it simmer for 5-10 minutes.
    Longer = stronger flavor. 5 min for mild, 10 min for intense.
  4. Pour into a mug through a strainer (or just leave the ginger in).
  5. Add honey and stir. Add a squeeze of lemon if you have it.

Veggie Egg Scramble
10
Minutes
High Protein
Good For
What it looks like: Fluffy yellow eggs mixed with green spinach, red tomatoes, bits of white/purple onion. Colorful and chunky.
What You Need
  • 3 eggs
  • Handful of spinach
  • 1/4 red onion, diced
  • 1 small tomato, diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Butter or oil
  • Salt & pepper
Steps
  1. Dice the onion and tomato into small pieces. Mince the garlic (chop it super fine). Rough chop the spinach if the leaves are big.
  2. Heat a pan on medium heat. Add butter or oil.
  3. Add onion first. Cook 2 minutes until it starts to soften.
  4. Add garlic. Stir for 30 seconds. Don't let it burn (it goes from perfect to burnt fast).
  5. Add tomatoes. Cook 1 minute.
  6. Add spinach. It will wilt down fast - just stir until it shrinks (30 seconds).
  7. Crack eggs directly into the pan. Stir everything together, breaking up the eggs as they cook.
  8. When eggs are just set (still slightly wet), take off heat. Season with salt and pepper.
    They keep cooking from residual heat, so take them off early.

Breakfast Burrito
12
Minutes

Good For
What it looks like: Rolled up tortilla, golden brown spots from the pan. Cut in half shows eggs, veggies, maybe cheese inside. Compact and portable.
What You Need
  • 2-3 eggs
  • 1 large tortilla
  • Handful of spinach
  • 1/4 red onion, diced
  • 1 small tomato, diced
  • Cheese (optional)
  • Salt & pepper
Steps
  1. Make the veggie scramble from the previous recipe (or just scrambled eggs if you want simple).
  2. Warm the tortilla. Either: microwave 15 seconds, or put it directly on the stove burner for a few seconds each side.
    Warm tortilla = doesn't crack when you roll it.
  3. Put the eggs in a line across the middle of the tortilla. Add cheese if using.
  4. Fold the sides in first (left and right). Then roll from bottom to top, keeping it tight.
  5. (Optional) Put the burrito seam-side down in a dry pan on medium heat for 1-2 minutes to seal and get it crispy.

What it looks like: Creamy, thick porridge. Topped with whatever you add. Should be thick enough that a spoon stands up in it, but not dry.
Basic Oatmeal
  1. Put 1 cup oats + 2 cups water (or milk) in a pot.
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat.
  3. Stir occasionally. Cook 5 minutes for regular oats, 10-15 for steel cut.
    Add a pinch of salt while cooking - makes it taste way better.
  4. When thick and creamy, take off heat. Add toppings.
Topping Ideas (Use What You Have)
  • Honey + Ginger: Drizzle honey, add minced fresh ginger
  • Savory: Fried egg on top, salt, pepper
  • Banana + Honey: Slice banana, drizzle honey
  • Cinnamon + Honey: Sprinkle cinnamon, drizzle honey
Overnight Oats (No Cooking)
  1. Put 1/2 cup oats + 1/2 cup milk in a jar or container.
  2. Add 1 tbsp honey. Stir.
  3. Put in fridge overnight (or at least 4 hours).
  4. Eat cold in the morning. Add toppings.

Veggie Garlic Rice
20
Minutes
Full Meal
Good For
What it looks like: White/slightly golden rice mixed with green spinach, red tomatoes, bits of onion. Colorful. Can see garlic pieces. Steam rising.
What You Need
  • 1 cup rice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 red onion, diced
  • Handful of spinach
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 2 tbsp butter or oil
  • Salt & pepper
  • Radish, sliced (for topping)
Steps
  1. Cook rice according to package (1 cup rice + 2 cups water, boil, then simmer 15-20 min covered).
  2. While rice cooks: heat butter/oil in a large pan on medium heat.
  3. Add onion. Cook 2 minutes.
  4. Add garlic. Cook 30 seconds (don't burn it).
  5. Add tomatoes. Cook 2 minutes until they soften.
  6. Add cooked rice to the pan. Stir everything together.
  7. Add spinach. Stir until wilted.
  8. Season with salt and pepper. Top with sliced radish for crunch.
    Add a fried egg on top for extra protein.

Quick Quesadilla
8
Minutes
Fast
Good For
What it looks like: Golden brown tortilla, crispy on outside. Cut into triangles. Cheese stretching when you pull pieces apart. Veggies visible inside.
What You Need
  • 2 tortillas (or fold 1 in half)
  • Cheese (any kind that melts)
  • Spinach
  • 1/4 red onion, sliced thin
  • Tomato, sliced thin
  • Butter or oil
Steps
  1. Heat a pan on medium heat. Add a little butter or oil.
  2. Put one tortilla in the pan.
  3. Add cheese on top of the tortilla (cover the whole thing).
  4. Add spinach, onion, tomato on one half.
    Don't overload it or it won't close properly.
  5. When cheese starts melting and bottom tortilla is golden (2-3 min), fold in half. Or put second tortilla on top.
  6. Flip and cook other side until golden (2-3 min).
  7. Slide onto cutting board. Let sit 1 minute (cheese is lava hot). Cut into triangles.
